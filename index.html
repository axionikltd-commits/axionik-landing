The problem

Brands are trapped in a marketplace economy — and the next channel is arriving without infrastructure.

The web's commerce rails were built for humans clicking through marketplaces. Those rails are starting to break under a new user: the agent. Brands are about to lose a second channel the same way they lost the first — unless the layer beneath gets built.

The protocol

Intent-Based Protocol Activation.
One integration. Any agent. Any brand.

IBPA is a neutral infrastructure layer — agent-agnostic and merchant-agnostic. A brand integrates once. Any compliant agent can then resolve intent, match a catalog, verify inventory, and authorize payment in a single agent-native flow.

01   Intent
User talks to their agent
"Reorder my running shoes." The agent captures intent in natural language — no storefront, no URL, no funnel.

Positioning

Everyone else is building a walled garden.
We're building the neutral rail.

The agent-commerce stack is being defined right now — by hyperscalers and payment networks, each locking merchants into a single agent or a single storefront. None of them is building what merchants actually need: a protocol they own a seat at.

Walled-garden approaches
Pick one agent, or pick one storefront.
Single-agent plugins force merchants to bet on one platform. Merchant-app stores limit transactions to one commerce backend. Both create lock-in, neither solves the underlying protocol problem.
Axionik
Any agent. Any catalog. One protocol.
Brands integrate once via IBPA and transact across every compliant agent — today's and tomorrow's. We don't take a cut of GMV. Merchants keep margin, own data, and retain the customer relationship.
